**Palindrome-containing strings**

Let F<sub>5</sub>(n) be the number of strings s such that:
- s consists only of '0's and '1's,
- s has length at most n, and
- s contains a palindromic substring of length at least 5.

For example, F<sub>5</sub>(4)&nbsp;=&nbsp;0, F<sub>5</sub>(5)&nbsp;=&nbsp;8, F<sub>5</sub>(6)&nbsp;=&nbsp;42 and F<sub>5</sub>(11)&nbsp;=&nbsp;3844.

Let D(L) be the number of integers n such that 5&nbsp;≤&nbsp;n&nbsp;≤&nbsp;L and F<sub>5</sub>(n) is divisible by 87654321.

For example, D(10<sup>7</sup>)&nbsp;=&nbsp;0 and D(5·10<sup>9</sup>)&nbsp;=&nbsp;51.

Find D(10<sup>18</sup>).
