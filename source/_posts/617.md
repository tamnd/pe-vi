## **Mirror Power Sequence**

For two integers $n,e>1$, we define a $(n,e)$-<i>MPS (Mirror Power Sequence)</i> to be an infinite sequence of integers $(a\_i)\_i\ge0$ such that for all $i\ge0$, $a\_{i+1}=\min(a^e\_i,n−a^e\_i)$ and $a\_i>1$.   
Examples of such sequences are the two (18,2)-MPS sequences made of alternating 2 and 4.

Note that even though such a sequence is uniquely determined by $n,e$ and $a\_0$, for most values such a sequence does not exist. For example, no $(n,e)$-MPS exists for $n<6$.

Define $C(n)$ to be the number of $(n,e)$-MPS for some $e$, and $D(N)=\sum\_{n=2}^NC(n)$. 

You are given that $D(10)=2$, $D(100)=21$, $D(1000)=69$, $D(10^6)=1303$ and D$(10^{12})=1014800$.

Find $D(10^{18})$.
***
## **Dãy lũy thừa gương**

$f(n) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^{n-k+1}$. Tính $\sum_{n=1}^{10^6} f(n) \pmod{10^9+7}$.
