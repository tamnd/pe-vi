**Pythagorean tiles**

Let (a, b, c) represent the three sides of a right angle triangle with integral length sides. It is possible to place four such triangles together to form a square with length c.

For example, (3, 4, 5) triangles can be placed together to form a 5 by 5 square with a 1 by 1 hole in the middle and it can be seen that the 5 by 5 square can be tiled with twenty-five 1 by 1 squares.

<center><img src="/resources/images/0139.png"></center>

However, if (5, 12, 13) triangles were used then the hole would measure 7 by 7 and these could not be used to tile the 13 by 13 square.

Given that the perimeter of the right triangle is less than one-hundred million, how many Pythagorean triangles would allow such a tiling to take place?

## **Gạch tam giác Pythagoras**

Tam giác Pythagoras $(a, b, c)$ có thể ghép bốn cạnh để tạo hình vuông $c \times c$. Nếu lỗ hổng giữa chia hết cạnh $c$, hình vuông có thể lát gạch hoàn toàn.

Với chu vi tam giác nhỏ hơn một trăm triệu, có bao nhiêu tam giác Pythagoras cho phép lát gạch như vậy?
