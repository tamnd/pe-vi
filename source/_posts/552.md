**Chinese leftovers II**

Let A<sub>n</sub> be the smallest positive integer satisfying  A<sub>n</sub> mod p<sub>i</sub> = i  for all 1 ≤ i ≤ n, where p<sub>i</sub> is the i-th prime.
For example A<sub>2</sub> = 5, since this is the smallest positive solution of the system of equations 
- A<sub>2</sub> mod 2 = 1 
- A<sub>2</sub> mod 3 = 2

The system of equations for A<sub>3</sub> adds another constraint. That is, A<sub>3</sub> is the smallest positive solution of
- A<sub>3</sub> mod 2 = 1 
- A<sub>3</sub> mod 3 = 2
- A<sub>3</sub> mod 5 = 3

and hence A<sub>3</sub> = 23. Similarly, one gets A<sub>4</sub> = 53 and A<sub>5</sub> = 1523.

Let S(n) be the sum of all primes up to n that divide at least one element in the sequence A.
For example, S(50) = 69 = 5 + 23 + 41, since 5 divides A<sub>2</sub>, 23 divides A<sub>3</sub> and 41 divides A<sub>10</sub> = 5765999453. No other prime number up to 50 divides an element in A.

Find S(300000).
