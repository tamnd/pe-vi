**Chocolate covered candy**

Phil the confectioner is making a new batch of chocolate covered candy. Each candy centre is shaped like an ellipsoid of revolution defined by the equation: b<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup> = a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>.

Phil wants to know how much chocolate is needed to cover one candy centre with a uniform coat of chocolate one millimeter thick.

If a=1 mm and b=1 mm, the amount of chocolate required is $\frac{28}{3}$π mm<sup>3</sup>.

If a=2 mm and b=1 mm, the amount of chocolate required is approximately 60.35475635 mm<sup>3</sup>.

Find the amount of chocolate in mm<sup>3</sup> required if a=3 mm and b=1 mm. Give your answer as the number rounded to 8 decimal places behind the decimal point.
