<b>Counting Capacitor Circuits</b>

An electric circuit uses exclusively identical capacitors of the same value C.
The capacitors can be connected in series or in parallel to form sub-units, which can then be connected in series or in parallel with other capacitors or other sub-units to form larger sub-units, and so on up to a final circuit.

Using this simple procedure and up to n identical capacitors, we can make circuits having a range of different total capacitances. For example, using up to n=3 capacitors of 60μF each, we can obtain the following 7 distinct total capacitance values: 

<center><img src="/resources/images/0155_capacitors1.gif"></center>

If we denote by D(n) the number of distinct total capacitance values we can obtain when using up to n equal-valued capacitors and the simple procedure described above, we have: D(1)=1, D(2)=3, D(3)=7 ...

Find D(18).

<em>Reminder :</em> When connecting capacitors C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> etc in parallel, the total capacitance is C<sub>T</sub>&nbsp;=&nbsp;C<sub>1</sub>&nbsp;+&nbsp;C<sub>2</sub>&nbsp;+...,
whereas when connecting them in series, the overall capacitance is given by:$\frac{1}{C\_T}=\frac{1}{C\_1}+\frac{1}{C\_2}+...$.
***
## **Đếm mạch tụ điện**

Dùng tối đa $n$ tụ điện giống hệt nhau kết nối nối tiếp hoặc song song, $D(n)$ là số điện dung tổng phân biệt có thể tạo ra.

Tìm $D(18)$.
