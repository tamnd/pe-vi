**Integer-valued polynomials**

It can be shown that the polynomial n<sup>4</sup> + 4n<sup>3</sup> + 2n<sup>2</sup> + 5n is a multiple of 6 for every integer n. It can also be shown that 6 is the largest integer satisfying this property.

Define M(a, b, c) as the maximum m such that n<sup>4</sup> + an<sup>3</sup> + bn<sup>2</sup> + cn is a multiple of m for all integers n. For example, M(4, 2, 5) = 6.

Also, define S(N) as the sum of M(a, b, c) for all 0 &lt; a, b, c ≤ N.

We can verify that S(10) = 1972 and S(10000) = 2024258331114.

Let F<sub>k</sub> be the Fibonacci sequence:
F<sub>0</sub> = 0, F<sub>1</sub> = 1 and
F<sub>k</sub> = F<sub>k-1</sub> + F<sub>k-2</sub> for k ≥ 2.

Find the last 9 digits of Σ S(F<sub>k</sub>) for 2 ≤ k ≤ 1234567890123.
