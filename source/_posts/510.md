**Tangent Circles**

Circles A and B are tangent to each other and to line L at three distinct points.
Circle C is inside the space between A, B and L, and tangent to all three.

Let r<sub>A</sub>, r<sub>B</sub> and r<sub>C</sub> be the radii of A, B and C respectively.

<center><img src="/resources/images/0510_tangent_circles.png"></center>

Let S(n)&nbsp;=&nbsp;Σ&nbsp;r<sub>A</sub>&nbsp;+&nbsp;r<sub>B</sub>&nbsp;+&nbsp;r<sub>C</sub>, for 0&nbsp;&lt;&nbsp;r<sub>A</sub>&nbsp;≤&nbsp;r<sub>B</sub>&nbsp;≤&nbsp;n where r<sub>A</sub>, r<sub>B</sub> and r<sub>C</sub> are integers.

The only solution for 0&nbsp;&lt;&nbsp;r<sub>A</sub>&nbsp;≤&nbsp;r<sub>B</sub>&nbsp;≤&nbsp;5 is r<sub>A</sub>&nbsp;=&nbsp;4, r<sub>B</sub>&nbsp;=&nbsp;4 and r<sub>C</sub>&nbsp;=&nbsp;1, so S(5)&nbsp;=&nbsp;4&nbsp;+&nbsp;4&nbsp;+&nbsp;1&nbsp;=&nbsp;9.

You are also given S(100)&nbsp;=&nbsp;3072.
Find S(10<sup>9</sup>).
***
