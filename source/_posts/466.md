**Distinct terms in a multiplication table**

Let P(m,n) be the number of <em>distinct</em> terms in an m×n multiplication table.

For example, a 3×4 multiplication table looks like this:

×&nbsp;|1&nbsp;|2&nbsp;|3&nbsp;|4&nbsp;
:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:
1&nbsp;|1&nbsp;|2&nbsp;|3&nbsp;|4&nbsp;
2&nbsp;|2&nbsp;|4&nbsp;|6&nbsp;|8&nbsp;
3&nbsp;|3&nbsp;|6&nbsp;|9&nbsp;|12     

There are 8 distinct terms {1,2,3,4,6,8,9,12}, therefore P(3,4) = 8.

You are given that:
P(64,64) = 1263,
P(12,345) = 1998, and
P(32,10<sup>15</sup>) = 13826382602124302.

Find P(64,10<sup>16</sup>).
