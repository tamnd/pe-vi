## **Integer sided equiangular hexagons**

Let $H$($n$) be the number of distinct integer sided <b>equiangular</b> convex hexagons with perimeter not exceeding $n$.
Hexagons are distinct if and only if they are not <b>congruent</b>.

You are given $H$(6)=1, $H$(12)=10, $H$(100)=31248.
Find $H$(55106).
