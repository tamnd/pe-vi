**Minimum values of the Carmichael function**

The <b>Carmichael function</b> λ(n) is defined as the smallest positive integer m such that a<sup>m</sup> = 1 modulo n for all integers a coprime with n.
For example λ(8) = 2 and λ(240) = 4.

Define L(n) as the smallest positive integer m such that λ(k)&nbsp;≥&nbsp;n for all k&nbsp;≥&nbsp;m.
For example, L(6) = 241 and L(100) = 20 174 525 281.

Find L(20 000 000). Give the last 9 digits of your answer.
***
## **Giá trị nhỏ nhất hàm Carmichael**

$f(n) = \min_{k \ge 1} \lambda(k) = n$ với $\lambda$ là hàm Carmichael. Tính $\sum_{n=1}^{10^6} f(n)$.
