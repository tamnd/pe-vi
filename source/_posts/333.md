**Special partitions**

All positive integers can be partitioned in such a way that each and every term of the partition can be expressed as 2<sup>i</sup>×3<sup>j</sup>, where i,j ≥ 0.

Let's consider only those such partitions where none of the terms can divide any of the other terms.
For example, the partition of 17 = 2 + 6 + 9 = (2<sup>1</sup>×3<sup>0</sup> + 2<sup>1</sup>×3<sup>1</sup> + 2<sup>0</sup>×3<sup>2</sup>) would not be valid since 2 can divide 6. Neither would the partition 17 = 16 + 1 = (2<sup>4</sup>×3<sup>0</sup> + 2<sup>0</sup>×3<sup>0</sup>) since 1 can divide 16. The only valid partition of 17 would be 8 + 9 = (2<sup>3</sup>×3<sup>0</sup> + 2<sup>0</sup>×3<sup>2</sup>).

Many integers have more than one valid partition, the first being 11 having the following two partitions.
11 = 2 + 9 = (2<sup>1</sup>×3<sup>0</sup> + 2<sup>0</sup>×3<sup>2</sup>)
11 = 8 + 3 = (2<sup>3</sup>×3<sup>0</sup> + 2<sup>0</sup>×3<sup>1</sup>)

Let's define P(n) as the number of valid partitions of n. For example, P(11) = 2.

Let's consider only the prime integers q which would have a single valid partition such as P(17).

The sum of the primes q &lt;100 such that P(q)=1 equals 233.

Find the sum of the primes q &lt;1000000 such that P(q)=1.

Đếm số nguyên tố $p$ dưới $500000$ mà số phân hoạch đặc biệt của $p$ là lẻ.
